AI is the Future of Mental Health Therapy! But!

October 7, 2024 by Dontae Whitely

Artificial intelligence (AI) technologies are revolutionizing mental health treatment through conversational interfaces. These systems, often driven by large language models, aim to bridge the gap between expert advice and accessibility. However, these AIs raise ethical concerns, including bias, unequal treatment of marginalized communities, and data privacy and confidentiality issues.

Therapists believe that people’s rights and dignity must be respected and their privacy, confidentiality, and self-determination must be preserved. Psychologists value each person's inherent worth and dignity as well as their freedom to privacy, confidentiality, and self-determination. Since AI systems in mental healthcare deal with extremely private and secret data, they may be vulnerable to data leaks that might reveal private patient information. According to Jeremiah Fowler (2024), Confidant Health, an AI-driven mental health service, suffered a significant data breach where personal information, including therapy notes and medical records, was leaked.
